Red Flag Behaviors

So what are red flag behaviors?

1. A warning signal.
2. Something that demands our attention.

A red flag is something we must pay attention to when getting to know someone. They are warning signs in a relationship. Not all red flags are non-negotiable or deal breakers. But, each red flag requires our attention and demands that we assess whether or not we should continue in the relationship.

1.

Blaming/refusing to take responsibility

Blaming/refusing to take responsibility

  • Speaking poorly about their ex
  • Always assuming the role of the victim
  • Saying comments like “all women/men are crazy”
  • Unwilling to foster self-awareness or ask for help
  • Refusing to admit fault

    Addiction issue that’s not being addressed

    Addiction issue that’s not being addressed

  • They become angry or different when they drink
  • They can’t have a meal or visit friends without drinking
  • They’re taking drugs (prescription or non) that leads to problematic behavior
  • They drive intoxicated
  • They actively (or secretly) smoke

    Controlling and/or violent behavior

    Controlling and/or violent behavior

  • Name-calling, yelling
  • Condescending, criticising, demeaning, belittling, blaming or shaming
  • Jealousy or passive-aggressiveness
  • Threatening the use of/exhibiting violence
  • Making unnecessary comments about where you spend your money
  • Lying or gaslighting you
  • Withholding/using sex as form of manipulation

    		•

    4.

    Emotional baggage that’s not being dealt with

    Emotional baggage that’s not being dealt with

  • They come on very strong before even getting to know you (also known as “love bombing”)
  • They’re needy, self-obsessed or otherwise attention-seeking
  • They avoid discussions relating to their past
  • They have a history of trauma that they’ve never sought help for or worked through
  • They have a history of dramatic, toxic relationships
  • They haven’t spent any significant time single (or they’re newly out of a relationship)
  • They’re chronic people-pleasers or conflict avoiders
  • They’re judgemental, homophobic, racist, bigot, or intolerant, closed minded

    		•

    The most productive way to deal with red flag behavior when it arises is to first and foremost acknowledge that it’s there. It’s important to take your time when getting to know someone because many red flag behaviors will be deal breakers, and we need to discover them before committing to a partner.
